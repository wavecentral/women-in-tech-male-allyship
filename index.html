About this Project

I built this to answer a question I kept asking myself.

I'm Mark Garcia, and in September 2025 I became co-lead of the Austin Women in Tech Impact Network. The network convenes quarterly, draws a hundred or more Austin technologists to each meeting, and is sponsored by Western Governors University — where I earned my own degree in Business Administration and IT Management.

For most of our history, our panels have featured prominent women advocating for women. That work is foundational, and it continues. But one number kept bothering me: roughly one in ten of our attendees is a man. If allyship is going to be anything more than a conversation among the already-convinced, we need the rest of the room.

This site is where I'm collecting what I'm learning. The research on what actually works. The books I'm reading and the notes I'm taking. The events our Impact Network has hosted and the ones coming up. If you're a man who wants to do this work better — or someone trying to bring a man you respect, is a friend, or work with into the conversation — I hope you find something useful here.

Recommended Reading

Books I'm Working Through

The short list of books that have most shaped how I think about this work. I'll publish my own notes and takeaways for each as I go.

Good Guys

How Men Can Be Better Allies for Women in the Workplace

W. Brad Johnson & David G. Smith

Written by a Naval Academy psychologist and a Johns Hopkins sociologist. Widely cited as the foundational text for practical male allyship.

My notes coming soon

Better Allies (2nd Ed.)

Everyday Actions to Create Inclusive, Engaging Workplaces

Karen Catlin

Catlin draws on 25 years as a tech VP at Adobe, and this fully revised second edition adds dozens of new scenarios. Concrete and tactical where a lot of allyship writing stays abstract.

My notes coming soon

Some Assembly Required (2nd Ed.)

A Networking Guide for Women

Marny Lifshen & Thom Singer

Written by an Austin marketing consultant and an Austin speaker-CEO. The book is grounded in the insight that networking works differently for women, and provides practical tools that adapt to individual styles rather than forcing a single script.

Thom Singer was our April 2026 fireside chat speaker — the book is how I got to know his work.
My notes coming soon

Why Women

The Leadership Imperative to Advancing Women and Engaging Men

Jeffery Tobias Halter

Halter is the former Director of Diversity Strategy at The Coca-Cola Company and founder of YWomen. His “Father of Daughter Initiative” offers one of the clearest entry points I've seen for male allyship, and the book frames the work in concrete business terms for skeptical male executives.

My notes coming soon

Athena Rising

How and Why Men Should Mentor Women

W. Brad Johnson & David G. Smith

The predecessor to Good Guys, focused on the mentorship-to-sponsorship pipeline that most women in tech report is where they lose ground.

My notes coming soon

Invisible Women

Data Bias in a World Designed for Men

Caroline Criado Perez

How a world built on male-default data fails women — in medicine, safety, product design, and code. Winner of the FT & McKinsey Business Book of the Year. Essential reading for anyone building technology that real humans use.

My notes coming soon

The Authority Gap

Why Women Are Still Taken Less Seriously Than Men, and What We Can Do About It

Mary Ann Sieghart

Backed by research across professions. Particularly useful for understanding meeting dynamics and whose ideas get credit. Includes interviews with Hillary Clinton, Janet Yellen, and Julia Gillard.
